Step1:

Users can upload compound names and standard inchiKey using excel file. After clicking on upload button, MICHA will extract all publicly available information for the compounds such as primary targets, other potent targets, clinical information and physiochemical information.

Step2:

After viewing the information returned by MICHA, users need to click on ‚ÄòNext‚Äô button to go to annotations page. On annotations page, users will need to fill-in experimental annotations for their compounds e.g. Tested cell-lines or patient samples, plates, mediums, assay formats, detection technologies, concentration ranges etc. Users will be able to see existing annotations for MICHA fairified protocols (if matching with user‚Äôs uploaded compounds).

Step3: Final result

Finally, an annotation report can be generated for the users. In addition, users may also browse the catalogue of drug sensitivity screen protocols which were already FAIRified by MICHA.
